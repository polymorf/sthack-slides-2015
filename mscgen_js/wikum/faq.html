Frequently asked questions

Online interpreter

Internet explorer support?

No, although in IE10 and up things appear to be working.

In our stats we see the majority of our visitors use chrome and firefox and - to a fare lesser extent - safari. The number of visitors using IE in the last year equaled two, one of them us wanting to confirm stats work in IE....

So, it seems there's no big demand for it. However, if supporting IE would be a piece of cake we would consider it. It is not. IE9 and lower don't support scalable vector graphics. IE10 does, in a way that is probably correct, but which is also a little off from how chrome, firefox, safari and even opera are doing it.

On the practical side, we do not have direct access to machines running windows 7 (needed for IE10 & higher) either.

Opera support?

Yep. The one visitor using Opera can now enjoy the interpreter as well.

Are you going to support styles/ skins?

No.

Rendering differences between mscgen_js and mscgen?

There are a few differences in how mscgen_js renders charts as compared to mscgen. Some of them are technologically based, some personally.

  • Entities have a box rendered around them (in mscgen they don't)
  • Text in entities automatically wraps (in mscgen only when explicitly forced with \n - but then again, in mscgen it generally doesn't have to fit in a box)
  • mscgen_js uses 9pt Helvetica (with the browser's idea of 9pt sans-serif as fall back), mscgen uses something else.
  • self refering arcs are not rendered as elipses
  • mscgen_js renders arrows on skewed lines at their angle as compared to the arc (mscgen always angles them to a horizontal line)
  • mscgen places a self refering arcs either on the right or on the left side, mscgen_js always places it on the right
  • Links in the svg are real links (in mscgen they're just blue; links are in a separate ismap)
  • ids get rendered as small text in square brackets instead of as superscript
  • mscgen_js does not generated imagemaps (ismap) and encapsulated postscript (eps).
